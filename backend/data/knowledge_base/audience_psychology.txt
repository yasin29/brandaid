AUDIENCE PSYCHOLOGY AND CONSUMER BEHAVIOR — MARKETING SIMULATION REFERENCE

Source: Consumer psychology research, Nielsen, Kantar, Edelman Trust Barometer, HubSpot 2023–2024

---

AUDIENCE SEGMENTS — BEHAVIORAL PROFILES

GEN Z (Born 1997–2012, Ages ~14–28)
- Primary platforms: TikTok, Instagram, YouTube Shorts, Snapchat
- Content preferences: Short-form video, authentic/unpolished content, behind-the-scenes
- Trust signals: Peer reviews, creator endorsements, social proof, transparency
- Red flags: Corporate/salesy tone, stock photos, forced diversity, scripted content
- Purchase drivers: Values alignment, FOMO, trends, peer influence, instant gratification
- Attention span on ads: 1.3 seconds before skip decision
- Response to discounts: High — deal-seeking behavior strong
- Brand loyalty: Low — will switch for better values or price
- CTR sensitivity: High creative quality required; boring ads get immediate swipes
- Avg conversion: Requires 3–5 touchpoints before purchase

MILLENNIALS (Born 1981–1996, Ages ~28–43)
- Primary platforms: Instagram, Facebook, YouTube, LinkedIn (professional)
- Content preferences: Informative, value-driven, aspirational lifestyle content
- Trust signals: Expert reviews, detailed product information, brand reputation
- Red flags: Overpromising, lack of transparency, no social proof
- Purchase drivers: Quality, convenience, brand trust, sustainability, reviews
- Response to discounts: Moderate — quality > price, but deals appreciated
- Brand loyalty: Moderate — loyal if brand delivers consistently
- Avg conversion: 2–4 touchpoints, responds well to email retargeting

GEN X (Born 1965–1980, Ages ~44–59)
- Primary platforms: Facebook, YouTube, LinkedIn, email
- Content preferences: Informative, detailed, comparison-focused
- Trust signals: Expert endorsements, established brand reputation, detailed specs
- Red flags: Gimmicks, unclear value proposition, social media pressure tactics
- Purchase drivers: Value for money, reliability, detailed information, convenience
- Response to discounts: High — ROI-focused buyers
- Brand loyalty: High — once trust is established, very loyal
- Avg conversion: Longer research phase (5–7 touchpoints)

BABY BOOMERS (Born 1946–1964, Ages ~60–78)
- Primary platforms: Facebook, YouTube, email, Google Search
- Content preferences: Clear, detailed, traditional formats — less video, more text
- Trust signals: Established brands, testimonials, phone/chat support availability
- Red flags: Jargon, overly fast-paced content, pressure tactics
- Purchase drivers: Reliability, trust, clear value, customer service quality
- Response to discounts: Moderate
- Brand loyalty: Very high

CORPORATE PROFESSIONALS / B2B BUYERS
- Primary platforms: LinkedIn, email, Google Search, industry publications
- Content preferences: Thought leadership, case studies, ROI data, whitepapers
- Trust signals: Industry credentials, client logos, measurable results, peer recommendations
- Red flags: Vague claims, no ROI data, consumer-style messaging
- Purchase drivers: ROI, risk reduction, peer recommendations, vendor stability
- Avg conversion: 6–12 month sales cycle with multiple stakeholders
- Decision-making: Committee-based, not individual

VALUE-CONSCIOUS BUYERS (Cross-demographic)
- Motivated primarily by price-to-value ratio
- High comparison shopping behavior
- Respond to: percentage discounts, price anchoring, "best value" framing
- Turn off: premium positioning without clear differentiation
- Platforms: Google Shopping, price comparison sites, Reddit, deal forums

EMOTIONAL TRIGGERS IN ADVERTISING

HIGH-RESPONSE TRIGGERS:
- Fear of missing out (FOMO): Creates urgency; effective for limited-time offers
- Social proof: "1 million customers" or user reviews increase conversion 18%–35%
- Reciprocity: Free trials, samples, or content gifts create psychological obligation
- Scarcity: "Only 3 left" boosts conversion 60%–70% when perceived as genuine
- Authority: Expert/celebrity endorsement lifts CTR 20%–35%
- Identity alignment: "People like you choose X" drives high engagement with target audiences

LOW-RESPONSE / BACKFIRE TRIGGERS:
- Pressure tactics: "Act NOW before it's too late" — increasingly distrusted
- Exaggerated claims: "The best in the world" without proof — skepticism spike
- Generic stock imagery: Reduces trust 15%–30% compared to authentic visuals
- Overly polished B-roll: Gen Z perceives as inauthentic

TRUST SIGNALS BY IMPACT

Very High Impact:
- Real customer video testimonials
- Star ratings with volume (4.5★ / 10,000 reviews)
- Security badges on checkout pages
- Money-back guarantee

High Impact:
- Press mentions / media logos
- Certifications and awards
- Before/after results
- Influencer endorsements (micro > macro for authenticity)

Moderate Impact:
- Company history / years in business
- Team photos / founder story
- Social media follower count

Low Impact (standalone):
- Generic "trusted by thousands" without specifics
- Award logos without context
